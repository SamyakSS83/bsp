Non-Fiction

Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind – Yuval Noah Harari

Question: Which continent does the author consider the origin of Homo Sapiens?
Answer: Africa.
Question: What is the name of the first fictional entity that Harari cites as an example of an "imagined order"?
Answer: Peugeot.

The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks – Rebecca Skloot

Question: What property of Henrietta is taken without her consent?
Answer: Her cells.
Question: What type of cancer was Henrietta Lacks diagnosed with?
Answer: Cervical cancer.

Thinking, Fast and Slow – Daniel Kahneman

Question: What are the two modes of thinking that Kahneman describes in Thinking, Fast and Slow?
Answer: System 1 (fast, automatic) and System 2 (slow, deliberate).
Question: What is the cognitive bias where people overestimate their ability to predict outcomes?
Answer: Hindsight bias.

A Brief History of Time – Stephen Hawking

Question: What does Hawking describe as the role of "imaginary time" in understanding the universe's origin?
Answer: It removes the singularity, making the universe finite but without boundaries.
Question: What does the "no-boundary proposal" suggest about the universe?
Answer: The universe has no boundaries in space-time, making it self-contained.
